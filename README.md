Climate Temperature Analysis in Mexico (1902‚Äì2011)

This project analyzes long-term temperature patterns across five major regions of Mexico using historical climatic data from 1902 to 2011. The study evaluates trends in minimum, maximum, and mean temperature, identifies regions with the strongest warming signals, and measures thermal amplitude to understand climate variability.

The results provide insights into how different regions are warming over time and highlight areas experiencing more extreme temperature behavior. These findings support decision-making in agriculture, urban planning, energy demand forecasting, and environmental risk management.
